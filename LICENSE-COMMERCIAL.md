# Commercial Licensing

YTtools is distributed under the GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 (AGPL-3.0). The full text of AGPL-3.0 is in [LICENSE](./LICENSE).

A commercial license is available for organizations whose intended use of YTtools is incompatible with AGPL-3.0.

## When you need a commercial license

You likely need a commercial license if you want to:

- Integrate YTtools into a proprietary product without releasing the proprietary product's source code under AGPL-3.0.
- Run a modified version of YTtools as a hosted service without making your modifications source-available under AGPL-3.0.
- Distribute YTtools as part of a closed-source software product.
- Avoid the source-disclosure obligations that AGPL-3.0 imposes on derivative works and network deployments.

## When you do not need a commercial license

You do **not** need a commercial license for:

- Personal use of YTtools, modified or unmodified.
- Internal use within your organization, provided you do not redistribute modified versions externally or expose modified versions over a network to users outside your organization.
- Academic research and publication.
- Contributing to YTtools itself.
- Forking YTtools and releasing your fork publicly under AGPL-3.0.

If you are unsure whether your use case requires a commercial license, contact us before deploying. We respond to most inquiries within two business days.

## How to obtain a commercial license

Email **wnichols@gmail.com** with the following information:

- Your organization name and headquarters location
- A brief description of your intended use of YTtools
- Whether the use is internal-only, customer-facing, or both
- Approximate scale (number of users, deployments, or instances)
- Timeline for deployment

We will follow up with pricing and a license agreement draft. Commercial licenses are typically annual subscriptions with optional multi-year terms.

## License terms

The commercial license grants the same code rights as AGPL-3.0, with the source-disclosure requirements removed. Specifically, a commercial licensee may:

- Modify YTtools without releasing modifications.
- Embed YTtools in proprietary products.
- Host modified versions as services.
- Sub-license to end users of the licensee's product (subject to terms in the specific agreement).

A commercial licensee may **not**:

- Redistribute YTtools as a standalone product to compete with the project.
- Sub-license to other organizations for their independent use.
- Remove copyright notices or attribution from the codebase.

The exact terms are set in the signed license agreement.

## Compatibility with the open source version

The commercial license covers the same codebase as the AGPL-3.0 distribution. There is no separate "enterprise" feature set; commercial licensees use the same code as the open source community. Improvements made by the open source community flow into the commercial product on the same release schedule.
